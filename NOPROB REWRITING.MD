NoProp breaks this chain.During training, you pre-compute a noisy version of the target embedding ($x_0$) for every single diffusion timestep $t$, resulting in a corrupted latent tensor ($x_t$).Instead of waiting for data to trickle up from lower layers, your input text context ($c$) and the noisy target ($x_t$) are broadcast directly to every layer/block simultaneously.Each layer acts as a local function $f_\theta(c, x_t, t)$ whose only job is to minimize a local L2 loss:$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{local}} = \| x_0 - f_\theta(c, x_t, t) \|^2$$Because the target is pre-allocated at every noise level, every single block computes its gradient and updates its weights in parallel via a local backward pass.2.
